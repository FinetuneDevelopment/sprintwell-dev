Mastering the first 10 days of keto

So, you have decided to enter into the keto diet. This is, for most people, a successful way of losing weight, but be prepared to take on the challenge. It is a whole new eating lifestyle and may go against everything that nutritionists advise.

Start as you mean to go on, remove from your cupboards, fridge and anywhere else you store food, all the items that you should not eat on a keto plan. It is best to start with sugary sweets, snacks and fizzy drinks, to rid yourself of any temptation when you come home starving from a day’s work. If it is not there, you cannot eat it!

If you are having a few days off from work, or it is a holiday weekend, these are the best times to start as you can plan your food and have the time to spend to ease into the diet. The first few days will always be the worst (although some people do not have any dramatic effects), so occupy yourself with very light exercise or something that takes your mind away from thinking about food.

Because your body is changing gear and substituting ketones for glucose, there are bound to be some symptoms/side effects that go with this transition.

Here is a timetable (nothing is set in stone, but this idea follows the way the keto diet can affect you), to get you through those first few difficult days. The timing for reaching ketosis varies from person to person, but in general around 10 days should have put you into ketosis, some will achieve it quicker and others may wonder after 10 days that nothing seems to be happening.

There are elements of the diet that not even scientists understand, but in general there are far more yays than nays surrounding keto. Many scientists have worked in collaboration with top medical professionals from all over the world to attempt to derive conclusions. Scientific trials have shown excellent results on lifelong illnesses such as Diabetes type II, neurological conditions such as swelling of the brain, but like all sciences, more in-depth research will continue.

Day 1 - Getting ready for nutritional ketosis

You are ready to go. Today may be a good day, and you feel little or no side effects at all. If this is the case, please do not think it is going to be a breeze, the next couple of days may well show that. You are about to undergo a big change, so take this opportunity of checking out what foods you have and read some recipes to get used to the safe ratios to use to put you into ketosis. Do not go mad thinking if you eat even less carbs you will lose weight faster, it does not work like that and you could harm yourself in the process. Variants are allowed but stick to the basic guidelines in those first few days.

Day 2 - You may feel a little foggy

You potentially will not feel great today, a bit foggy, a few aches and pains, maybe even nausea. These are the first symptoms of keto flu, which means your body has already started the process. Just keep focus on why you are doing this, and comfort yourself in the knowledge that this will go away. Drink plenty of water, but do not go crazy with gym workouts or running. Get some rest, as Day 3 is imminent and this could be when you come down to earth and feel really lousy.

Day 3 Hang in there - it’s going to get better

Well, be prepared for the potential onslaught of flu-like symptoms. Some even describe it as the worst day of my life. Whilst a little dramatic, yes, you will feel terrible (again some people may get away with it lightly, but not many) and it could be the first of your thoughts of quitting. Let your body and how you feel take the lead if you are lethargic or weak, rest as much as possible. Do not overtask yourself at all. Fats are now being used totally for fuel, so no surprise that your system is reacting.

Day 4 - Fat is your friend

Could well be the turning point for you, fats are now more of a friend than an enemy. You may still feel lightheaded, not quite right, but the worst is over in most cases. Busy yourself with homemaking, batch cook some meals and peruse some recipes for ideas. Keep taking it easy, but you can take a little light exercise such as a walk in the fresh air.

Days 5 and 6 - You’ll be over the worse

Whilst you may not have hit the peak of full ketosis just yet, you should be feeling so much better and your body will be used to burning off fat for energy, even though you will feel on good form, best still not to overdo it. You can venture into testing your ketone levels if you have invested in urine strips, blood tests or other method, but giving it a week is preferable. You have not quite managed to conquer the world yet, but it is close.

Day 7 and 8 Back to feeling yourself - more or less

You should be feeling better all the time and more or less feeling yourself. Your body is almost used to the changes in diet, the keto flu should be well and truly on its way out. If you feel you have seriously improved over the last couple of days, you may well have cracked it. The best has yet to come!

Days 9 and 10 - Blood Sugar stabilised

You have an excellent chance of feeling a 100% now, although some participants of the keto diet take a little longer. You can do some more exercise and your energy levels should be at their best. If you were one of those people that always had a bit of a slump after lunch, you will be excited to hear that this may well have gone away and you feel more invigorated. Celebrate with pampering yourself as a reward.

Your blood sugar is no longer spiking or dropping as this is caused by too many carbs, well they have gone now, so feeling lazy and wanting a bit of a snooze should be non-existent in the early afternoons. You may not even feel particularly hungry, but it is important to follow the plan.

Drink plenty of water, as now you are probably in ketosis, and ketones can make you dehydrated in a short period of time. Sip constantly through the day, and even if you do not feel hungry, do eat. Starving is not part of this diet.

It is time to enjoy your achievement and reap the benefits of a new you. You can check your ketones, weigh yourself without fear and get on with your life. Congratulations, we know it has been tough, but you made it!

Weight loss on keto

We are frequently asked how much weight will I lose in the first 10 days of keto? This is not possible to answer definitively as all bodies are different. It would be unusual not to lose a few pounds, but it can be as much as around 10lbs or so, a great result, so persevere.
