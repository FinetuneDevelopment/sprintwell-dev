Diet

What is keto flu?

Keto flu – you must have heard or read about it if you are interested in starting a keto diet. It is a set of potential problems (from mild to a little more debilitating) that are symptomatic of this particular diet.

If you enter ketosis, it is likely to occur in the first few days to a week if you are sticking strictly to the plan. When you consider that you may be making vast decisions about losing weight, something is bound to happen. You must not worry; it is quite normal and many people suffer from it in the early stages. It may last a day or so (most likely), but it can drag on a little longer. If you are unfortunate enough for it to continue, try to stay with it, but if it begins to alarm you and your symptoms are more than those listed, do consult a medical professional.

We recommend that you start your ketosis programme when you will not be working (such as the weekends) or socialising where food and drink will be a temptation! Try to shop for a few days so that you are organised and do not panic buy. You could even batch cook a few meals, so you are ready to go.

Are there any specific symptoms?

Yes, there are typical symptoms of keto flu, varying in having strong symptoms, to maybe only one that is just making you feel a little off.

You may experience one or two of these, or even all of them. When you radically change your diet, whichever one you choose, if you can still notice some of the above symptoms, maybe if you cut back on processed foods or choose to follow an anti-inflammatory and/or an elimination diet (FODMAP), this may solve it.

In very extreme cases, keto flu can last up to a month of your new regime, but your first 7-10 days is more likely.

Can I alleviate any of these symptoms?

There are ways to get you through this period that you can try, but they will not necessarily work for everyone. First and foremost, keep yourself hydrated with plenty of water, as you would with the normal variety of flu. Try to get enough rest and sleep, even though lack of sleep may be one of your symptoms.

If you must take time off work as you really do not feel good, use that time to snatch some sleep. Eat well, even if you do not feel like it, as food that is nutritionally dense will keep your body working and start to make you feel better.

If exhaustion is your major problem, keep on resting even if you cannot sleep. Even when resting, keep plenty of water next to you and top up your hydration level regularly.

Above all, don’t waste time and money on supposed cures for keto flu. This is just a moneymaking and opportunist situation that many companies are promoting to make some fast funds. After serious investigation, these will not work and you have wasted your money. There is no scientific proof whatsoever.

Do I need to visit my doctor?

Should your symptoms be extremely severe, you may need to visit your doctor and be checked out for other causes. Some people have reported a thyroid imbalance due to a temporary and sudden adjustment to your level of carbohydrate intake, but this again, is not unexpected.

Another knock-on effect from a decrease in carbs in your diet, may be a change in electrolyte effectiveness (this is the main cause of dehydration). As you are on a very low carb diet, your insulin levels do not rise as high as they would normally after food consumption. Therefore, it is important to continue eating through keto flu, even if you do not feel like it. Whilst chemists have a range of OTC> (over the counter) drinks to combat loss of electrolytes, these more than likely contain sugar, so best not to use those if you intend to remain on the keto diet.

Just keep your mind on the overall plan and the weight loss you will see once keto flu has been banished!
